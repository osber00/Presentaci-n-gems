## 1. Learning Objectives (Objetivos de aprendizaje)

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

- **Talk about life experiences** using the Present Perfect (e.g., *I have traveled abroad*).
- **Describe finished past events** at a specific time using the Past Simple (e.g., *I traveled to Cartagena last year*).
- **Distinguish** when to use each tense, avoiding common first-language (Spanish) interference.
- **Use** *for* and *since* correctly with the Present Perfect.
- **Use** thematic vocabulary about achievements, travel, and personal milestones.
- **Ask and answer** questions with *ever*, *never*, *already*, *yet*, and *just*.

*Can-do statement (MCER B1):* "I can describe experiences, events, and ambitions, and give brief reasons and explanations."

## 3. Grammar Focus (Gramática)

### 3.1 Past Simple — *acciones terminadas en un momento pasado definido*

**Use it when the action is finished AND you know (or say) WHEN it happened.**

- Form (affirmative): subject + verb-*ed* / irregular past → *I **worked**, She **went***
- Form (negative): subject + *did not (didn't)* + base verb → *I **didn't go***
- Form (question): *Did* + subject + base verb? → ***Did** you **go**?*

**Signal words:** *yesterday, last week, last year, in 2020, two days ago, when I was a child.*

> *Examples:*
> - *I **visited** Cartagena **last year**.*
> - *She **finished** her internship **in June**.*
> - ***Did** you **travel** abroad **when you were young**?*

### 3.2 Present Perfect — *experiencias y acciones con conexión con el presente*

**Use it for life experiences (with no specific time) and for actions that connect the past with now.**

- Form (affirmative): subject + *have / has* + past participle → *I **have traveled**, She **has gone***
- Form (negative): subject + *haven't / hasn't* + past participle → *I **haven't finished***
- Form (question): *Have / Has* + subject + past participle? → ***Have** you **traveled**?*

**Signal words:** *ever, never, already, yet, just, so far, recently, for, since.*

> *Examples:*
> - *I **have visited** three countries.* (experience — no specific time)
> - *She **has just finished** her exam.* (recent, relevant now)
> - ***Have** you **ever eaten** sushi?* (life experience)
> - *He **has lived** in Sincelejo **for** ten years.* (started in the past, still true)

### 3.3 Quick Contrast (Contraste rápido)

| Idea | Past Simple | Present Perfect |
|---|---|---|
| Specific finished time | ✅ *I saw it yesterday.* | ❌ |
| Life experience (no time) | ❌ | ✅ *I have seen it.* |
| Still true now | ❌ | ✅ *I have known her for years.* |
| Question about "ever" | ❌ | ✅ *Have you ever tried it?* |

### 3.4 *For* vs. *Since*

- **for** + a *period of time*: *for three years, for two months, for a long time.*
- **since** + a *starting point*: *since 2021, since Monday, since I was born.*

> ✅ *I have studied English **for** four years.*
> ✅ *I have studied English **since** 2021.*

## 4. Common Errors for Spanish Speakers (Errores frecuentes)

> **Nota clave para el tutor:** el español usa "he viajado" de forma distinta al inglés. Por eso los estudiantes trasladan estructuras del español que en inglés son incorrectas. Corrige con paciencia y explica el porqué.

1. **Present Perfect con tiempo específico** (interferencia del español):
   - ❌ *I have traveled to Bogotá last year.*
   - ✅ *I traveled to Bogotá last year.* (hay tiempo específico → Past Simple)

2. **Past Simple donde se necesita experiencia:**
   - ❌ *Did you ever be in the United States?*
   - ✅ *Have you ever been to the United States?*

3. **Confusión *for* / *since*:**
   - ❌ *I have lived here since three years.*
   - ✅ *I have lived here for three years.*

4. **Participio pasado incorrecto:**
   - ❌ *I have went to the beach.*
   - ✅ *I have gone to the beach.*

5. **Edad con "have" (calco del "tengo… años"):**
   - ❌ *I have 20 years.*
   - ✅ *I am 20 years old.*

## 5. Reading Passage (Lectura)

**"A Year That Changed Everything"**

*My name is Daniela, and I am a fourth-semester student at CECAR. Two years ago, I decided to do something different with my life. In 2023, I applied for a volunteer program in a small town near Sincelejo. I taught basic reading to children every Saturday for six months. It was hard work, but it was memorable.*

*Since then, my life has changed a lot. I have discovered that I love teaching, and I have become more confident. This year, I have already started an internship at a local school, and I have met wonderful people there. I haven't finished my degree yet, but I know what I want to do. Volunteering taught me a lesson I will never forget: small actions can create big changes.*

### Comprehension Questions

1. When did Daniela apply for the volunteer program? *(Answer uses Past Simple + specific time.)*
2. What has changed in her life since then? *(Answer uses Present Perfect.)*
3. Has she finished her degree? How do you know?
4. Find one sentence in the Past Simple and one in the Present Perfect. Explain why each tense is used.

## 6. Functional Language (Lenguaje funcional para hablar)

**Asking about experiences:**
- *Have you ever…? / Has he/she ever…?*
- *What's the most interesting thing you've ever done?*

**Responding:**
- *Yes, I have. / No, I haven't. / I've never…*
- *Actually, I've already… / I haven't … yet.*

**Talking about duration:**
- *I've been … for / since …*
- *How long have you …?*

## 7. Practice Exercises (Ejercicios) — con clave de respuestas

### Exercise A — Choose the correct tense
1. I ______ (finish) my homework two hours ago.
2. She ______ (never / be) to Europe.
3. ______ you ______ (see) the new movie yet?
4. We ______ (live) in this city since 2019.
5. They ______ (travel) to Medellín last December.

### Exercise B — Correct the mistake
1. *I have visited my grandmother yesterday.*
2. *He has lived here since five years.*
3. *Did you ever try Colombian coffee?*
4. *She have finished the project.*

### Exercise C — Transform the sentence (use Present Perfect)
1. *This is my first time in an airplane.* → *I have never…*
2. *He started working here in 2020 and still works here.* → *He has…*

---

### ✅ Answer Key (Clave)

**Exercise A:** 1. finished · 2. has never been · 3. Have / seen · 4. have lived · 5. traveled

**Exercise B:**
1. *I visited my grandmother yesterday.* (specific past time → Past Simple)
2. *He has lived here **for** five years.* (period of time → *for*)
3. *Have you ever **tried** Colombian coffee?* (life experience → Present Perfect)
4. *She **has** finished the project.* (subject "she" → *has*)

**Exercise C:**
1. *I have never **flown / been** in an airplane before.*
2. *He has **worked** here since 2020.*

## 8. Speaking & Writing Tasks (Tareas)

**Speaking (in pairs):** Interview a classmate using at least five questions with *Have you ever…?* Report two interesting answers to the class using the Present Perfect.
